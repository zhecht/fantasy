Expert 
Derek Lofland
FantasyPros		Kansas City is 8th against fantasy running backs, but this is probably the best offense they have played on the season. Ingram should be a low-end RB1 this week.Mixon is coming off a poor showing and the Bills are 13th against fantasy running backs. I view Mixon as more of a RB2 this week.
Andrew Seifter
FantasyPros		As the clear lead back in one of the league's best offenses, Ingram is an every-week RB2, but there are some concerns. He isn't a true bell cow and remains lightly involved as a pass-catcher, which could be an issue this week if the Ravens are forced into a pass-heavy approach against the Chiefs. The game has a very high projected score line, which is a positive for all involved, but Ingram will be set up better when the Ravens are more likely to be playing with a lead.Mixon entered Week 2 banged up with a sprained ankle, so perhaps he deserves a bit of a pass for his anemic stat line against the 49ers. Still, the Bengals may continue to rotate Gio Bernard in fairly frequently to protect Mixon's health, at least in the short term. Bernard certainly appears to be the team's passing down back at the moment, which could be an issue for Mixon's playing time as six-point underdogs at Buffalo. He should be approached as a risky, low-end RB2.
